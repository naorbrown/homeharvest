Overview & Varieties

Microgreens are vegetable and herb seedlings harvested when the first true leaves appear. That means 7 to 14 days from seed to plate.

Microgreens contain 4 to 40 times the nutrients of mature plants.

Grow them on any countertop, any season, no outdoor space needed.

Best beginner varieties:

  • Sunflower — Nutty and crunchy. Soak seeds 8–12 hours; ready in 10–12 days.
  • Radish — Spicy and fast. Ready in 5–7 days, no soaking needed.
  • Broccoli — Mild, nutrient-dense, rich in sulforaphane. Ready in 8–10 days.
  • Pea shoots — Sweet and tender. Soak overnight; ready in 10–14 days and can be harvested twice.
  • Wheatgrass — For juicing, not salads. Ready in 10–12 days.
Growing Requirements
Light Indirect light or windowsill
Water Mist 2x daily with spray bottle
Growing Medium 1 inch potting soil, coconut coir, or hemp mat
Temperature 65–75°F (18–24°C)

Skip direct sunlight — it wilts delicate seedlings. Bright indirect light or a windowsill is ideal.

Use a standard 10×20 inch nursery tray or any shallow dish with drainage.

Step-by-Step Growing Guide
  1. Soak seeds overnight (if needed). Sunflower, pea, and wheatgrass need 8–12 hours. Broccoli and radish can go in dry.
  2. Prepare your tray. Add 1 inch of moistened soil, coir, or hemp mat. Damp, not waterlogged.
  3. Spread seeds densely. Single layer, almost no gaps. A 10×20 tray takes about 1–2 oz of seed.
  4. Cover for 2–3 days (blackout phase). Place a tray or cardboard on top. Mist once daily.
The blackout phase forces seedlings to push upward, developing strong stems.
  1. Uncover and expose to light. Seedlings should be ½–1 inch tall and pale yellow. They green up within 24 hours near a window or grow light.
  2. Mist daily until harvest. Keep the medium moist, never soaked. Bottom watering prevents mold best.
  3. Harvest at 1–2 inches tall. Cut just above the soil line when the first true leaves appear. Rinse and eat immediately.
Common Problems & Solutions
Mold or fuzzy white growth
Caused by overwatering or poor airflow. Reduce misting, add a small fan, and switch to bottom watering.
Leggy, thin growth
Not enough light. Move closer to a window or add a grow light — they need 4–6 hours of indirect light daily.
Uneven germination
Uneven seed spread or inconsistent moisture. Ensure a single, even layer on uniformly damp medium.
Slow growth in cold temperatures
Below 60°F, growth stalls. Place trays in your warmest room or use a seedling heat mat.
Seeds not germinating
Old seeds lose viability. Buy seeds labeled for microgreen use and store in a cool, dry place.
Best Spaces for Microgreens

Microgreens need almost zero space. Two ideal setups:

Windowsill

A single tray on a windowsill produces a continuous supply. Rotate trays weekly for steady harvests.

Indoor Setup

A shelving unit with grow lights lets you stack multiple trays vertically. Scale to any production level.
