Overview & Varieties

Root vegetables grow underground, so you can’t see problems until harvest. The critical rule: direct sow only — taproots fork, stunt, or die when transplanted.

Radishes are the fastest vegetable you can grow — ready in 22–28 days.

Each root vegetable has different timing and depth requirements:

  • Radishes — Ready in 22–28 days, only 4–6 inches of soil depth needed. Winter “Daikon” types take 50–60 days and need 12+ inches.
  • Carrots — 60–75 days, need 12+ inches of loose, rock-free soil. For containers, use short varieties like “Parisian” or “Chantenay” (6–8 inch depth).
  • Beets — 55–65 days, 8–10 inches deep. Each seed is a cluster of 2–4 seeds, so thinning is essential.
  • Turnips — 40–55 days, 8–10 inches deep. “Hakurei” is mild and crisp enough to eat raw.
Growing Requirements
Light 6+ hours direct sun
Water Consistent, even moisture
Soil Deep, loose, rock-free
Temperature 50–75°F (10–24°C)
Soil quality matters more for root vegetables than any other crop — carrots need 12+ inches of loose, rock-free soil.

Rocks and compaction cause forking and stunted roots. Raised beds with potting soil and compost are ideal.

Step-by-Step Growing Guide
  1. Prepare deep, loose soil. Loosen to 12 inches for carrots, 8–10 for beets/turnips, 4–6 for radishes. Remove every rock and clump.
  2. Remove rocks and break up clumps. Sift the top 12 inches through a ½-inch screen for carrots. Any obstacle causes forking or splitting.
  3. Sow seeds at the proper depth. Radish: ½″ deep, 1″ apart. Carrot: ¼″ deep, ½″ apart. Beet: ½″ deep, 2″ apart. Turnip: ¼″ deep, 1″ apart. Carrots take 14–21 days to sprout — mark the rows.
  4. Keep soil moist during germination. Mist gently daily — strong spray dislodges seeds. Cover carrot beds with damp burlap to retain moisture.
  5. Thin seedlings to prevent crowding. At 2 inches tall: radishes 2″ apart, carrots 2–3″, beets 4″, turnips 4–6″. Snip at soil level with scissors — pulling disturbs neighbors.
  6. Water deeply 2–3 times per week. Irregular watering causes cracking and woody texture. Mulch with straw to retain moisture.
  7. Harvest when the shoulder shows above soil. Pull radishes at marble size — they turn pithy fast. Loosen soil with a fork before pulling to avoid snapping roots.
Thinning Guide

Thinning is the #1 maintenance task for root vegetables. Crowded roots produce small, misshapen results.

  • Radishes: Thin to 2 inches apart at 1 inch tall. Do it in the first week or you get all leaf, no root.
  • Carrots: Thin twice — to 1 inch apart at 2″ tall, then to 2–3 inches apart at 4″ tall. Use the second thinnings as baby carrots.
  • Beets: Thin to 4 inches apart at 3″ tall. Keep the strongest seedling from each cluster and snip the rest.
  • Turnips: Thin to 4–6 inches apart at 2″ tall. Closer spacing gives more baby turnips; wider spacing gives fewer, larger roots.
Common Problems & Solutions
Forked or misshapen roots
Caused by rocks, compacted soil, or fresh manure. Prevention only: prepare deep, loose, stone-free soil before planting.
Splitting or cracking
Caused by inconsistent watering — the root swells faster than the skin can expand. Maintain even moisture and harvest promptly when mature.
Poor or no germination
Carrot seed viability drops fast — always use fresh seed. Cover with vermiculite instead of soil to prevent crusting.
Root maggots (small white worms in the root)
Fly larvae that tunnel into roots. Cover beds with floating row cover right after planting to block them.
Bolting (sending up a flower stalk)
Triggered by heat or long days — the root becomes woody and inedible. Grow in spring or fall, and succession plant radishes every 10 days.
Best Spaces for Root Vegetables

Root vegetables need depth more than surface area.

Backyard

Raised beds are ideal — fill with deep, loose soil for perfect roots every time. In-ground works if soil is sandy and loose.

Balcony

Deep containers (12+ inches) for carrots, or standard pots for radishes and baby beets. Grow bags work well.
