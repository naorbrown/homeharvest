Overview & Varieties

Herbs are the best starting point for any home grower. Minimal space, fast growth, daily culinary payoff.

One windowsill basil plant outproduces a grocery store bunch weekly.

Two categories to know:

  • Annual herbs — Basil, cilantro, dill, parsley. Fast from seed, one season of heavy production. Succession plant every 3–4 weeks for nonstop supply.
  • Perennial herbs — Rosemary, thyme, mint, oregano, chives. Come back yearly with less maintenance. Always grow mint in its own container.
Growing Requirements
Light 6–8 hours direct sun
Water When top inch is dry
Soil Well-draining potting mix
Temperature 60–80°F (15–27°C)
Overwatering is the #1 killer — herbs prefer slightly dry over wet.

Always use pots with drainage holes. Let soil dry between waterings.

Step-by-Step Planting Guide
  1. Choose your container. Any pot 6–8 inches deep with drainage holes. One herb per 6" pot, or combine in a 12" planter.
  2. Fill with potting mix. Use indoor potting mix, not garden soil. Fill to 1 inch below the rim.
  3. Plant seeds or starts. Seeds: scatter on surface, cover ¼" deep, mist gently. Starts: match hole to root ball, firm soil, water thoroughly.
  4. Position for light. South-facing windowsill is ideal. LED grow light at 12–16 hrs/day works if natural light is limited.
  5. Water consistently. Finger-test 1" into soil daily. Dry? Water until it drains. Moist? Wait a day.
  6. Feed monthly. Half-strength liquid fertilizer once a month. Over-fertilizing reduces flavor.
  7. Harvest regularly. Start when plants have 3+ sets of true leaves. Never take more than one-third at once.
Harvest Timing & Technique

Harvest correctly and your herbs keep producing for months.

  • Basil: Pinch above a leaf node to double production. Remove flower buds immediately.
  • Cilantro: Cut outer stems at the base. Succession plant every 2–3 weeks — bolts fast in heat.
  • Mint: Cut freely — nearly impossible to over-harvest. Pinch tips for bushy growth.
  • Parsley: Cut outer stalks at the base. Leave the center crown to keep producing.
  • Rosemary: Snip 3–4" stem tips only. Won’t regrow from old woody growth.
Common Problems & Solutions
Leggy, stretched growth
Not enough light. Move to brighter spot or add a grow light. Minimum 6 hours direct sun.
Yellow leaves at the base
Overwatering. Let soil dry more between waterings. Check that saucer isn’t holding water.
Wilting despite moist soil
Root rot. Repot in fresh, dry soil and cut watering frequency.
Sudden flowering (bolting)
Heat or stress. Remove flower buds immediately. Succession plant cilantro so young plants are always ready.
Small white flies or sticky leaves
Whiteflies or aphids. Spray diluted neem oil or wipe with soapy water. Isolate affected plants.
Best Spaces for Herbs

Herbs thrive in almost any space.

Windowsill

The ideal herb setup. South-facing window, 2–3 pots, daily harvesting.

Balcony

Railing planters or grouped containers. More sun exposure than indoors.

Indoor Setup

Grow lights let you grow herbs anywhere year-round, regardless of windows.
