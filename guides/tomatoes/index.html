Overview & Varieties

Tomatoes are the crown jewel of home food growing.

One cherry tomato plant in a 5-gallon container can produce 200+ fruits per summer.

Store-bought tomatoes are picked green and gas-ripened. A vine-ripened homegrown tomato is a completely different experience.

Two growth types to know:

  • Determinate (bush) — Fixed height (3–4 ft), fruits in a 2–3 week burst, then done. Best for containers. Varieties: Patio, Bush Early Girl, Roma.
  • Indeterminate (vine) — Keeps growing and producing all season. Needs staking, more space, but way more total fruit. Varieties: Sungold, Sweet 100, Brandywine.

Best beginner varieties:

  • Cherry/grape — Most productive and forgiving. Sungold and Sweet 100 are prolific.
  • Patio — Bred for containers. Patio Princess and Tumbling Tom stay compact, no staking needed.
  • Slicers — Full-sized. Better Boy and Early Girl are reliable. Need 5-gallon pots and stakes.
  • Heirlooms — Brandywine, Cherokee Purple — incredible flavor, lower yields. Not for beginners.
Growing Requirements
Light 8+ hours direct sun (non-negotiable)
Water Deep watering every 2–3 days
Soil Rich potting mix with compost
Temperature 65–85°F (18–29°C)
Under 6 hours of direct sun = drastically less fruit. Shady space? Pick a different crop.

Use at least a 5-gallon pot. Undersized containers mean constant watering stress.

Step-by-Step Growing Guide
  1. Start seeds indoors 6–8 weeks before last frost. Plant ¼" deep, keep soil 70–80°F. Germination in 5–10 days.
  2. Pot up when true leaves appear. Transplant to 4" pots. Bury the stem up to the first leaves — tomatoes root along buried stems.
  3. Harden off for one week. Gradually expose to outdoor conditions, starting with 1–2 hours daily.
  4. Transplant when nights stay above 50°F. Bury two-thirds of the stem. One plant per 5-gallon container.
  5. Stake or cage at planting time. Don’t wait. Indeterminate types need 6+ foot stakes.
  6. Prune suckers on indeterminate types. Pinch off shoots in the stem-branch “armpit” when under 2 inches. Don’t prune determinate varieties.
  7. Water deeply at the base, every 2–3 days. Never on leaves. Consistency is everything once fruit sets.
Pruning & Training Guide

These apply to indeterminate varieties only:

  • Remove suckers below the first flower cluster. Directs energy to fruit and improves airflow.
  • Limit to 1–3 main stems. Cherry types: 2–3 stems. Slicers: 1–2 stems.
  • Remove yellowing lower leaves. Improves airflow, reduces disease.
  • Top the plant 4 weeks before first frost. Forces energy into ripening existing fruit.
Common Problems & Solutions
Blossom end rot
Black sunken spot on fruit bottom. Caused by inconsistent watering, not calcium. Water deeply and mulch.
Tomato hornworms
Large green caterpillars. Hand-pick them. White cocoons on one? Leave it — parasitic wasp eggs.
Early blight
Brown ringed spots on lower leaves. Remove affected leaves, mulch, water at base only.
Fruit cracking
Heavy watering after dry spell. Keep watering consistent. Mulch 3–4 inches deep.
Flower drop
Temps above 90°F or below 55°F at night. Wait it out or use shade cloth.
Best Spaces for Tomatoes

Tomatoes need maximum sun but can thrive in surprisingly small footprints:

Balcony

5-gallon containers on a sun-drenched, south-facing balcony. Cherry and patio types excel here.

Backyard

Raised beds or in-ground rows with full sun. Grow the big heirlooms you can’t fit on a balcony.
