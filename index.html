ü´ò

Seed Saving

Keep your best genetics. Reduce seed costs to zero.

  • ‚Üí Why it matters ‚Äî Saving seeds from your strongest plants creates varieties adapted to your specific conditions.
  • ‚Üí Beginner seeds ‚Äî Start with tomatoes, peppers, beans, and lettuce. These are self-pollinating and easy to collect.
  • ‚Üí Storage ‚Äî Dry seeds thoroughly. Store in labeled envelopes in a cool, dark place. Most last 2‚Äì5 years.

Composting

Turn kitchen waste into the richest soil amendment you can get.

  • ‚Üí Kitchen scrap compost ‚Äî A countertop bin collects coffee grounds, vegetable peels, and eggshells. Add to a tumbler or pile weekly.
  • ‚Üí Worm bins ‚Äî Vermicomposting works indoors, produces rich castings, and handles scraps continuously. Ideal for apartments.
  • ‚Üí Outdoor piles ‚Äî Layer greens (nitrogen) and browns (carbon). Turn occasionally. Finished compost in 2‚Äì6 months.
